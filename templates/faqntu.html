FAQ

 

What is NTU?

 

Turbidity (measured in NTU) refers to the cloudiness or haziness of water caused by suspended particles. High turbidity levels indicate poor water quality, which can harm pets' health. Turbid water may contain harmful bacteria, viruses, and pollutants, increasing the risk of gastrointestinal infections and other illnesses. For pets, clean water is essential to maintaining hydration and overall well-being. Regular monitoring of turbidity is crucial to ensuring safe drinking water. If NTU levels exceed 5, it may signal contamination, making it unsafe for pets to consume.
